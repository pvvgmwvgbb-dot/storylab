# Teacher Narration

## 1. Earth is born

A long, long time ago, there was no classroom, no city, no forest, and no dinosaur.

There was only space, dust, gas, and a young Sun.

Slowly, small pieces came together. They became bigger rocks. The bigger rocks became baby Earth.

Baby Earth was not blue and calm. It was hot, red, and full of fire.

## 2. Earth becomes a living world

After a very long time, Earth cooled down. Water appeared. Oceans formed. Tiny life began in the water.

Much later, plants and animals appeared. And much, much later, dinosaurs walked on Earth.

## 3. The museum portal

You are in a museum. You see a dinosaur fossil.

Suddenly, the fossil starts to glow.

A blue circle opens under your feet.

The room spins.

You fall through time.

## 4. Dinosaur chase

You open your eyes.

The air is hot. The plants are huge. The ground shakes.

Boom. Boom. Boom.

A dinosaur sees you.

You run.

## 5. The cliff

You reach a high ledge. It is ten blocks high.

There are many square blocks on the ground, but no stairs.

You must build the stairs yourself.

The dinosaur is getting closer.

## 6. Case A: one block at a time

If you can only climb one block higher each step, the staircase must rise like this:

1 block, then 2 blocks, then 3 blocks, all the way to 10 blocks.

So the question is:

1 + 2 + 3 + ... + 10 = ?

Do not answer yet. Look at the shape.

It is a staircase.

## 7. The rectangle trick

Now copy the same staircase.

Turn the copy around.

Put the two staircases together.

Now we do not see a messy staircase anymore. We see a rectangle.

The rectangle has 10 rows.

Each row has 11 blocks.

So two staircases have 10 * 11 = 110 blocks.

One staircase has half of that:

110 / 2 = 55.

## 8. Case B: two blocks at a time

Now imagine your shoes upgrade.

Each step can rise two blocks.

The rows become:

2, 4, 6, 8, 10.

This is still a pattern. Each row grows by the same amount.

2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + 10 = 30.

## 9. Escape

You build the stairs.

You climb to the ledge.

The dinosaur stops below.

It cannot reach you.

You are safe.

## 10. Reflection

Back in the museum, you write in your notebook:

A formula is not magic.

A formula is a pattern that we have learned to see.
