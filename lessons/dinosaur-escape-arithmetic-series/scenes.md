# Scenes

These are screen-level visual anchors. Keep each scene image-first and text-light.

## Scene 01: Space dust

Visual:
- dark space
- young Sun
- dust and rocks moving together
- tiny baby Earth

Purpose:
Open the story at the largest time scale.

On-screen text:
`A long time ago...`

## Scene 02: Hot young Earth

Visual:
- red hot Earth
- lava
- volcano smoke

Purpose:
Show that Earth was not always blue and calm.

On-screen text:
`Baby Earth was hot.`

## Scene 03: Dinosaur world

Visual:
- giant plants
- volcano in the distance
- several dinosaurs

Purpose:
Move from Earth history into adventure.

On-screen text:
`Much later... dinosaurs lived here.`

## Scene 04: Time portal

Visual:
- museum floor
- dinosaur fossil
- glowing blue portal

Purpose:
Transport the learner into the story.

On-screen text:
`The fossil glows.`

## Scene 05: Chase

Visual:
- child running
- dinosaur behind
- dust clouds
- shaking ground marks

Purpose:
Create urgency.

On-screen text:
`Run!`

## Scene 06: Cliff and blocks

Visual:
- high ledge labeled 10 blocks high
- pile of square blocks
- dinosaur far behind

Purpose:
Create the need for math.

On-screen text:
`Build a way up.`

## Scene 07: One-step staircase

Visual:
- rows of square blocks: 1, 2, 3, ..., 10
- no formula yet

Purpose:
Let the learner see the staircase before calculating.

On-screen text:
`How many blocks?`

## Scene 08: Rectangle trick

Visual:
- yellow staircase
- blue copied staircase rotated
- together forming a 10 by 11 rectangle

Purpose:
Make the formula visible.

On-screen text:
`Two staircases make a rectangle.`

## Scene 09: Two-step staircase

Visual:
- rows of blocks: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10
- jumping path marks

Purpose:
Compare a second arithmetic pattern.

On-screen text:
`What if each jump is 2 blocks?`

## Scene 10: Escape and return

Visual:
- child on high ledge
- dinosaur below
- museum notebook overlay

Purpose:
Reward and abstract.

On-screen text:
`A formula is a pattern we can see.`
